Why Twilio? (Quick Context)

I went through a bunch of options before landing on this — Skype, CallHippo, JustCall. Here's the thing: most of them cost significantly more, and in the early stage you don't need all that extra setup and features.

Skype also doesn't exist for this use case anymore. JustCall and CallHippo are solid tools but they're priced for teams, not solo operators testing the waters. Twilio keeps it lean — pay as you go, full control, and it works.

01
Create Your Twilio Account

Head to twilio.com and sign up. When they ask for your role, just pick Freelancer, Small Business, or Self-Employed — honestly doesn't matter much which one you choose.

Verify your email and mobile number (they'll ask for 2FA). Standard stuff, takes 2 minutes.

02
Business Registration — No GST Needed

Twilio asks for business verification but you don't need GST for this. Here's the hack:

  • Go to the Udyam Registration Portal (it's free, government site)
  • Register as a micro/small business — takes about 5 minutes
  • Download the certificate
  • Use this to verify your Twilio business profile ✅
💡

Udyam registration is completely free and gives you a legit business document on the spot. No CA, no fees, no waiting.

03
Buy a Number & Set Up Billing

Go ahead and buy a US or Canada number — local numbers work best for cold calling. Enter your card details and put in the minimum $20.

⚠️

Important: During payment, you'll see an "Auto-recharge" checkbox. Check it carefully.

💰

Twilio charges roughly $0.013–$0.014 per minute of call duration. Your $20 gets you a lot of dial time — just track it so you're not caught off guard.

04
Get a Wired Headset — Learned This the Hard Way

Real talk — I started with Bluetooth earphones and I was getting prospects saying "I can't hear what you're saying, man" and hanging up. I thought it was my script. It wasn't. It was the audio.

Bluetooth adds lag, muffling, and interference. On a cold call, that kills your first impression instantly. So I searched for the best wired earphones, and picked up the Apple EarPods — clean mic, zero lag, works great.

  • Apple EarPods (wired) — what I personally use, solid mic quality
  • JBL Tune 310C — haven't tried it myself but looks promising from reviews
  • Any wired headset with a good mic — the keyword is wired
🎧

Prioritise mic quality over everything. The prospect doesn't care what you look like — they care if they can hear you clearly. Wired = no excuses.

05
Get STIR/SHAKEN "A" Status (Anti-Spam)

This is the most important step most people skip. If your calls are getting flagged as spam before the prospect even picks up — this is why.

Go to Monitor → Call Logs and check your STIR Status. If it's B or C, your calls are likely being marked as potential spam.

  • Go to Account → Trust Hub → SHAKEN/STIR Trusted Calling (Voice)
  • Add your purchased number there
  • This upgrades your attestation to Level A
⚠️

Don't see your number to add? This happens. Check if your phone number is properly associated with your account first — go to Phone Numbers → Manage → Active Numbers and confirm it's showing up. Then try Trust Hub again.

📧

Twilio support usually responds within 24 hours. If you email them for verification, attach your website domain if you have one — it makes your profile look legit and speeds things up.

✅

Level A means your calls show up clean on the prospect's screen. No "Spam Risk" label. This alone can double your connect rate.

06
Set Up Your Auto Dialer

Now plug everything together:

  • Install the Twilio Auto Dialer Chrome extension
  • Enter your Account SID and Auth Token (find these on your Twilio dashboard)
  • Set the "From" number to your verified Level A number

And that's it — you're ready to start dialing US prospects from India at a fraction of what any other setup costs.

💳

The Twilio Auto Dialer Chrome extension has a free trial — after that it costs around ₹1,300 for 3 months. Cheap enough that one booked call more than pays for it.

📈

Best practice: Gradually increase your dials per day — don't blast 100+ calls on day one. Sudden high volume can get your number flagged as spam or trigger a Twilio account review. Start slow, ramp up over days, and protect the number you worked to get to Level A.
